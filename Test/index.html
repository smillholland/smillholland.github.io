About

I am a data-driven dynamicist interested in a broad range of problems in exoplanetary science. In particular, I study the formation and evolution, orbital architectures, and physical properties of exoplanets, and I am especially motivated by observational mysteries. I employ a synergy of celestial mechanics theory, numerical simulations, and statistical methods. For more information, check out my Research page.

I completed my PhD in Astronomy at Yale University in May 2020, where I was an NSF Graduate Research Fellow from 2017-2020. My thesis is titled "Data-Driven Dynamics of Planetary Systems". I spent my first year of graduate school at University of California Santa Cruz, before I transferred to Yale to follow my advisor, Professor Greg Laughlin.
